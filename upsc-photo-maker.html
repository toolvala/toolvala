What is UPSC Photo Maker?

The UPSC Photo Maker is a specialized online tool created for candidates applying for Union Public Service Commission examinations such as Civil Services (IAS, IPS, IFS), NDA, CDS, and CAPF. UPSC has strict photo guidelines, and even a small mistake like missing Date of Photo or incorrect background can lead to rejection.

Why UPSC Photo Guidelines Are Strict?

UPSC verifies identity using uploaded photographs during all stages of the examination, including prelims, mains, and interview. To prevent impersonation, UPSC mandates that the photograph must be recent and should clearly display the candidate's face along with the Date of Photo.

UPSC Passport Size Photo Requirements

  • Dimensions: 350 × 350 pixels
  • Background: Plain white
  • File size: Up to 300 KB
  • Recent photograph (within 3 months)
  • Name and Date of Photo clearly visible

Date of Photo (DOP) vs Date of Birth (DOB)

UPSC notifications usually mention Date of Photo (DOP). However, some forms may ask for DOB. This UPSC photo maker allows you to choose the correct option using a dropdown to avoid mistakes.

How to Use UPSC Photo Maker?

  1. Upload your passport-size photograph
  2. Enter your name (optional but recommended)
  3. Select DOP or DOB
  4. Choose the date
  5. Auto white background is applied
  6. Face is auto-centered
  7. Adjust quality to control KB size
  8. Download UPSC-ready photo

Auto White Background for UPSC

UPSC requires a clean white background. Any colored or patterned background may result in rejection. This tool automatically fills the background with pure white.

Face Detection Auto Crop

Where supported by the browser, a face detection feature helps with correct face positioning and head size ratio. Where it isn't supported, the photo is center-cropped to the square frame without any face-aware adjustment — both methods preserve the original aspect ratio so your photo is never stretched or squashed.

UPSC KB Indicator

A real-time KB indicator helps you stay within UPSC's allowed file size. Green means safe, yellow means caution, and red indicates a high chance of rejection.

Is This UPSC Photo Maker Safe?

Yes. This tool runs entirely in your browser. No image is uploaded to any server, ensuring maximum privacy.
