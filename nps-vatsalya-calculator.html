3. Understanding NPS Tier 1 and Tier 2 Logic

When you use an **NPS Tier 1 Calculator**, you are planning for the core pension pillar. NPS Vatsalya accounts are primarily Tier 1, meaning they are designed for retirement security with locked-in periods that prevent impulsive spending. Conversely, an **NPS Tier 2 Calculator** is used for voluntary savings accounts that offer high liquidity but no tax benefits. For your child, the Vatsalya account ensures a disciplined foundation. 4. Eligibility and Key Requirements

The eligibility rules for NPS Vatsalya are inclusive, ensuring every Indian household can participate in the nation's growth:

  • Age Requirement: The beneficiary must be a minor (below the age of 18 years).
  • Citizenship: The child must be an Indian citizen. Non-resident Indians (NRIs) are also eligible to open accounts for their children to build a future base in India.
  • Guardian Oversight: A parent or legal guardian must open and operate the account until the child attains majority.
  • KYC Compliance: The guardian must provide valid documentation, including Aadhaar and PAN details.

5. Contribution Rules and SIP Flexibility

NPS Vatsalya offers flexibility that traditional child insurance plans lack. Our **NPS SIP Calculator** logic accounts for:

  • Minimum Entry: You can keep the account active with just ₹1,000 per financial year.
  • Upper Limit: There is no maximum limit, allowing for aggressive high-net-worth estate planning.
  • Mode of Payment: Choose between monthly SIPs or yearly lumpsums based on your cash flow.
  • Asset Choice: Guardians can choose between Equity (up to 75%), Corporate Debt, and Government Securities.

6. The NPS Tax Benefit Calculator Logic

8. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What happens when the child turns 18? ▼
On the 18th birthday, the account automatically converts to a regular NPS Tier-1 account. The child then becomes the primary subscriber and must complete their own KYC process.
2. Can I withdraw the money early? ▼
Partial withdrawals (up to 25% of contributions) are permitted after 3 years for specific reasons like higher education or marriage. However, the account is designed to stay invested until age 60.
3. How much pension will my child get? ▼
The pension depends on the 'Annuity Corpus' (minimum 40% of maturity). Based on our projections, a monthly investment of ₹5,000 from birth can result in a monthly pension of over ₹5 Lakhs by age 60.
4. Is the fund manager selection important? ▼
Yes. You can choose between PFM managers like LIC, SBI, HDFC, or ICICI. You have the right to change your fund manager once every year.
5. Is NPS Vatsalya better than Sukanya Samriddhi? ▼
Sukanya Samriddhi is limited to girls and has a fixed interest rate. NPS Vatsalya is gender-neutral and market-linked (Equity), which historically offers much higher long-term returns.

No. Everything is calculated in your browser RAM. We do not store your personal financial data. Your privacy is 100% guaranteed.
