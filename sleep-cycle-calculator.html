Understanding Sleep Cycles

What Is a Sleep Cycle?

A sleep cycle is a complete progression through different stages of sleep—light sleep (stages 1-2), deep sleep (stage 3), and REM (rapid eye movement) sleep. This natural cycle typically lasts about 90 minutes. During a complete cycle, your brain processes memories, restores energy, and consolidates learning.

How the Sleep Cycle Calculator Works

Our calculator uses scientific sleep research to determine your optimal sleep times. It assumes an average of 15 minutes to fall asleep and calculates multiple 90-minute cycles. Choose between calculating from your wake time or bedtime based on your schedule.

Why Sleep Cycles Matter

Waking in the middle of a sleep cycle often causes sleep inertia—that groggy, disoriented feeling that can last for hours. By aligning your sleep with natural cycles, you improve mental clarity, memory, mood, and overall health.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long is one sleep cycle exactly?

A: Most adults have sleep cycles of approximately 90 minutes, though this can range from 80-100 minutes depending on age and genetics.

Q: How many sleep cycles do I really need?

A: Most adults need 4-6 complete cycles per night. Five cycles (7.5 hours) is considered ideal, though individual needs vary.

Q: Does this calculator work for everyone?

A: The 90-minute cycle is an average guideline. Individual needs vary due to age, genetics, and lifestyle. Use this as a starting point and adjust based on how you feel.

Q: What if I can't follow the exact times?

A: Aim to get within 30 minutes of the calculated times. Even approximate alignment with your cycles will improve how you feel.
