The Biological Impact of Screen Time

To appreciate why you need an eye strain prevention tool, you must understand the two primary biological factors at play:

1. The Blink Rate Drop

Normally, humans blink about 15-20 times per minute. Blinking spreads a fresh layer of "tear film" across the cornea, keeping it moist and clear. When we concentrate on a screen, we blink only 5-7 times per minute. This results in the tear film evaporating, causing the burning sensation known as "Dry Eye."

2. Blue Light and Contrast

Digital screens emit high-energy visible (HEV) blue light. While blue light is natural during the day, excessive exposure at close range can cause "flicker" and glare, reducing visual contrast and forcing the brain to work harder to process images. Over time, this contributes significantly to tension headaches and blurred vision.

Ergonomic Best Practices for Eye Health

While our 20-20-20 rule calculator helps you manage time, your physical setup is equally important. Follow these 5 guidelines:

  • The Arm's Length Rule: Your screen should be approximately 20 to 28 inches away from your eyes (about an arm's length).
  • The 15-Degree Tilt: Position your monitor so the center is about 15 to 20 degrees below eye level. This ensures you are looking slightly downward, which exposes less of the eyeball to the air, reducing evaporation.
  • Reduce Glare: Position your screen away from windows or use a matte screen filter to prevent light reflections that cause squinting.
  • Brightness Balance: Ensure your screen brightness matches the brightness of your room. A bright screen in a dark room is a recipe for severe strain.
  • Font Size: If you find yourself leaning forward to read, increase the font size in your browser or OS settings. Your posture and eyes will thank you.
