Complete Guide to Square Feet and Dismil Conversion

Land measurement in India is a complex tapestry of units that change as you cross state borders. While the standard metric system (Square Meters, Hectares) is used for official government records, regional units like Dismil (also spelled as Decimal or Decimel) remain the de facto standard for real estate transactions in Eastern India. If you are buying land, building a house, or calculating property taxes in states like Bihar, Jharkhand, West Bengal, or Odisha, understanding the relationship between Square Feet (sq ft) and Dismil is crucial.

This comprehensive guide will explain the definitions, the mathematical formula, historical context, and practical applications of these units.

What is a Square Foot?

The Square Foot (sq ft) is an imperial unit of area. It is defined as the area of a square with sides of 1 foot in length. Despite the global shift toward the metric system, Square Feet remains the most popular unit for measuring residential properties, apartments, and office spaces across India.

  • Symbol: sq ft or ft¬≤
  • Relation to Acre: 1 Acre = 43,560 sq ft
  • Usage: Universal (Real Estate, Architecture, Interior Design).

What is a Dismil (Decimal)?

The Dismil (often referred to as Decimal in West Bengal and Bangladesh) is a traditional unit of land measurement. It essentially represents one-hundredth of an acre. The term "Decimal" comes from the word "Deci," implying a tenth or hundredth part.

It is widely used in the rural and semi-urban areas of Eastern India for agricultural land and plot measurements. In local Patwari (land record) documents in Bihar and Jharkhand, you will frequently see land area denoted in Acres and Dismils.

  • Symbol: Dec or Dismil
  • Relation to Acre: 1 Acre = 100 Dismil
  • Primary Usage: West Bengal, Jharkhand, Bihar, Odisha, and Bangladesh.

The Conversion Formula: Square Feet to Dismil

The mathematical relationship between these two units is fixed based on the definition of an acre. Here is the breakdown:

1 Acre = 43,560 Square Feet

1 Acre = 100 Dismil

Therefore:

1 Dismil = 435.6 Square Feet

How to Convert SQFT to Dismil

To convert an area from Square Feet to Dismil, divide the area value by 435.6.

Formula: Area in Dismil = Area in Sq Ft / 435.6

Example: You have a plot of 1500 sq ft.
1500 / 435.6 = 3.44 Dismil.

How to Convert Dismil to SQFT

To convert an area from Dismil to Square Feet, multiply the area value by 435.6.

Formula: Area in Sq Ft = Area in Dismil √ó 435.6

Example: You are buying 5 Dismil of land.
5 √ó 435.6 = 2,178 sq ft.

Why is Land Measurement Confusing in India?

India's land measurement system is a mix of the metric system (introduced post-independence) and the traditional systems used during the Mughal and British eras. While Square Feet is standard for construction (plinth area), land deals often involve units like:

  • Katha/Bigha: Used in North and East India. The size of a Katha varies from district to district. For example, a Katha in Patna might be 1361 sq ft, while in some other parts of Bihar it is 720 sq ft.
  • Guntha: Used in Maharashtra and Karnataka.
  • Gaj (Square Yard): Popular in Delhi, Punjab, and Haryana.
  • Cent: Used in South India (Kerala, Tamil Nadu). 1 Cent is remarkably similar to 1 Dismil (1 Cent = 435.6 sq ft).

Because units like "Katha" vary, units like Dismil and Square Feet are preferred for legal documents because their values (1 Dismil = 435.6 sq ft) are standardized and do not change regardless of location.

Comparison: Dismil vs. Katha

In Bihar and Jharkhand, "Katha" is the most common unit. However, since the Katha varies, the Dismil is used to standardize the value.

Typically:

  • In some regions: 1 Katha = 3.125 Dismil (approx 1361 sq ft).
  • In others: 1 Katha = 4 Dismil.

Always check the local conversion rate if dealing in Katha, but stick to the calculator above for Dismil to Sq Ft as it is constant.

Tips for Measuring Land Accurately

  1. Check the Deed: Always look for the measurement in the "Sale Deed" or "Khatian". It will usually be in Decimal/Dismil or Hectares.
  2. Use a Surveyor (Amin): In Eastern India, land surveyors are called "Amins". They use chains (Gunter's chain) to measure land. 1 Gunter's Chain is 66 feet long. 10 square chains equal 1 Acre.
  3. Verify Boundaries: Physical possession often differs from paper possession. Measure the physical boundary walls using a tape measure in feet, calculate the square footage, and then use our tool to convert it to Dismil to see if it matches the papers.
  4. Account for Road Widening: If your land is near a road, ensure the measurement excludes any government setbacks.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Is 1 Dismil equal to 1 Decimal?

Yes. Dismil and Decimal are the same unit. "Dismil" is the phonetic pronunciation often used in Hindi and Bengali speaking regions, derived from the English word "Decimal".

2. How many Square Feet are in 1 Decimal?

There are exactly 435.6 square feet in 1 Decimal (Dismil).

3. How is Dismil different from Cent?

Mathematically, they are the same. 1 Cent (used in South India) = 435.6 sq ft. 1 Dismil (used in East India) = 435.6 sq ft. Both represent 1/100th of an acre.

4. How many Dismil make 1 Acre?

100 Dismil make 1 Acre.

5. Can I use this calculator for land in Bihar and West Bengal?

Absolutely. This calculator uses the standard conversion rate (435.6) which is valid for land registration in Bihar, Jharkhand, West Bengal, and Odisha.

6. How do I calculate the area of an irregular shaped land?

For irregular shapes, divide the land into geometric shapes like triangles and rectangles. Calculate the area of each shape in square feet, sum them up, and then enter the total square feet into this converter to get the value in Dismil.
