Electronic Signature (eSign) vs. Digital Signature

It is important to understand the terminology. This tool creates an Electronic Signature (eSign).

  • Electronic Signature: This is a visual representation of your signature (an image) placed on a document. In many jurisdictions (like the US E-SIGN Act and EU eIDAS), this is legally binding for most commercial agreements, leases, and contracts.
  • Digital Signature (Cryptographic): This involves a digital certificate ID (like a USB token) to cryptographically lock the document. This is required for specific high-security government tenders or filings.

For 99% of daily use cases‚Äîleases, offers letters, invoices, and permission slips‚Äîthe Electronic Signature generated by this tool is sufficient and legally recognized.
