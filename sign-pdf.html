Electronic signature vs. digital signature

Electronic signature (what this tool makes)

  • Valid for most contracts, leases, offer letters, and invoices
  • Created in seconds, no certificate or ID verification needed
  • Works the same on any device or PDF reader
  • Free and instant

Cryptographic digital signature

  • Requires a certificate from a recognized Certificate Authority
  • Usually needed for government filings or regulated submissions
  • Often paid, and tied to a verified identity
  • Provides cryptographic proof of integrity

The Complete Guide to Signing PDFs Online with Toolvala.in

Printing a document just to sign it, scan it, and email it back has always been a strange amount of friction for something so routine. Whether you're a freelancer closing a deal, a landlord sending a lease, an HR manager sending an offer letter, a student submitting a declaration, or a small business approving a purchase order, Toolvala.in's PDF Signer lets you sign directly on screen and download the finished file in under a minute — and it's built for all of these cases equally, not just one.

🔒 Security by design: Your PDF is read and modified entirely inside your browser using JavaScript. Toolvala.in has no server that receives, stores, or even briefly sees your file. Closing the tab clears everything.

Three ways to create your signature

  • Draw it: Use a mouse, finger, or stDisp draw your usual signature, in your choice of ink color.
  • Type it: Type your name and pick a handwriting-style font — useful when drawing with a mouse feels clumsy.
  • Upload it: Use a photo or scan of your real signature, ideally on a plain background.

Placing it exactly where it belongs

Instead of guessing with abstract presets, the tool shows you the actual page you're signing. Drag your signature to the right spot, resize it with the slider, and use the quick corner presets if you just need a standard placement like bottom-right. You can add the same signature — or initials — to several pages of one document before downloading a single combined file.

Electronic signatures vs. digital signatures

An electronic signature is a visual mark showing intent to agree to a document, and it's what this tool produces — legally sufficient for most everyday agreements in most jurisdictions. A digital signature is a different, cryptographic concept involving certificates from a recognized authority, typically reserved for regulated government or legal filings. Most people signing contracts, leases, or forms only need the former.
