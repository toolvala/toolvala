The Ultimate Guide to Indian Land Measurement Units

In the world of Indian real estate, navigating through land measurement units can feel like traveling through different countries. Every region has its own specific unit. While measuring a flat in Mumbai uses "Square Feet," buying agricultural land in Bihar uses "Dismil" and "Katha," and purchasing a plot in Chennai involves "Grounds."

This tool is designed to unify these confusing units into one simple interface. Below, we break down the most common units used in India and answer the specific confusion about rounding numbers.

1000 Square Feet to Dismil: Solving the Precision Confusion

Many users ask: "Is 1000 sq ft equal to 2.295684 Dismil or 2.2957 Dismil?"

The confusion arises from rounding. Let's look at the math:

  • The Constant: 1 Dismil = 435.6 Square Feet.
  • The Calculation: 1000 √∑ 435.6.
  • The Raw Result: 2.295684113865932...

So, which is correct?

  • 2.295684 is the precise answer (rounded to 6 decimal places). Use this for official surveys where high accuracy is needed.
  • 2.2957 is the standard answer (rounded to 4 decimal places). This is generally acceptable for buying/selling land as the difference (0.00001 Dismil) is physically negligible (less than a square inch).

Definitions of Common Units

1. Dismil (Decimal)

Commonly used in Bihar, Jharkhand, West Bengal, Odisha, and Bangladesh.

  • Definition: 1/100th of an Acre.
  • Conversion: 1 Dismil = 435.6 Sq Ft.

2. Cent

Commonly used in Kerala, Tamil Nadu, and Karnataka.

  • Definition: Mathematically identical to Dismil.
  • Conversion: 1 Cent = 435.6 Sq Ft.
  • Note: 100 Cents = 1 Acre.

3. Katha (Cottah)

Used in Bihar, West Bengal, and Assam.

Warning: The size of a Katha varies significantly by district. However, the "Standard" measurement usually accepted in Eastern India is:

  • Conversion: 1 Katha ‚âà 1361.25 Sq Ft (Standard).
  • In Dismil: 1 Katha ‚âà 3.125 Dismil.

4. Bigha

Used across North India (UP, Bihar, Punjab, Haryana).

Like Katha, Bigha varies wildly. In Punjab, a Bigha is small; in Bihar, it is large.

  • Standard (East India): 1 Bigha = 20 Katha.
  • Conversion: 1 Bigha ‚âà 27,225 Sq Ft.

5. Ground

Exclusively used in Tamil Nadu (Chennai real estate).

  • Conversion: 1 Ground = 2,400 Sq Ft.
  • Usage: Often used for residential plots. A "Half Ground" is 1,200 Sq Ft.

6. Guntha

Used in Maharashtra, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh.

  • Conversion: 1 Guntha = 1,089 Sq Ft.
  • Relation: 40 Gunthas = 1 Acre.

7. Are

Used in Maharashtra and government records (Metric System).

  • Conversion: 1 Are = 100 Square Meters ‚âà 1,076.39 Sq Ft.
  • Relation: 100 Are = 1 Hectare.

How to Measure Irregular Land?

Most land plots are not perfect rectangles. To get the Square Feet value to enter into this calculator:

  1. Divide the land map into geometric shapes (Triangles and Rectangles).
  2. Calculate the area of each shape individually.
  3. Add them together to get total Square Feet.
  4. Enter that total into our tool to convert it to your local unit (Dismil/Katha).
