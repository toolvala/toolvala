Why Do Government Exams Require Name and Date on Photos?

The Staff Selection Commission (SSC) and Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) introduced mandatory photo guidelines to prevent impersonation and ensure the photograph is recent. By requiring the Date of Photo (DOP) printed clearly on the image, the authorities can verify that the candidate's appearance matches the photo provided.

Usually, the notification states that the photograph should not be more than 3 months old from the date of the notification. Without a printed date, your application may be marked as "Provisional" or rejected during document verification.

Understanding the Difference: DOP vs. DOB

Many students get confused between DOB and DOP. It is critical to choose the correct one using our tool:

  • DOB (Date of Birth): This is your birthday. You rarely print this on a photo unless creating a specific ID card or School Form.
  • DOP (Date of Photo): This is the date the picture was clicked. 99% of government exams require DOP to prove the photo is recent.

Pro Tip: If you are using an older photo but look exactly the same, many candidates generate a current date (within 3 months) and print it on the photo using our tool. However, we advise using a freshly clicked photo for safety.

Common Mistakes to Avoid While Uploading Photos

Even after adding the name and date, candidates often make errors that cause issues. Ensure you avoid these:

  1. Blurry Text: If you compress the image too much (e.g., below 10KB), the text strip becomes unreadable. Keep it above 20KB.
  2. Inverted Images: Do not upload a selfie that mirrors text. The name on the photo must read Left-to-Right.
  3. Goggles and Hats: Ensure you are not wearing a cap or dark glasses. Spectacles are allowed only if there is no glare on the eyes.
  4. Wrong Background: Most exams require a white or light-colored background. If your background is dark, remove it before using this text tool.
