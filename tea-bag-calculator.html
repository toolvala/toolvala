Why Tea Brewing Accuracy Is Important

Tea brewing is not just about placing a tea bag in hot water. Different teas react differently to water temperature and steeping time. Incorrect brewing can result in bitter, watery, or flavorless tea. Using a tea brewing calculator removes guesswork and guarantees a balanced cup every time.

Tea Types and Brewing Guidelines

Black Tea

Black tea is bold and full-bodied. It requires boiling water and a longer steeping time to extract rich flavors. One tea bag per cup is usually sufficient.

Green Tea

Green tea is light and refreshing. Using boiling water can destroy antioxidants and cause bitterness. Cooler water and shorter brewing time are essential.

Herbal Tea

Herbal teas are caffeine-free and require longer steeping to extract natural flavors and health benefits.

Chai Tea

Chai is an Indian spiced tea prepared with milk, water, and spices. It requires strong boiling and longer steeping for full flavor infusion.
