Technical Details: How Image Compression Works

Image compression algorithms work by eliminating redundant or unnecessary image data. JPEG compression uses discrete cosine transformation to separate image data into different frequency components, then discards high-frequency details that are less noticeable to the human eye. PNG compression uses lossless DEFLATE algorithm, finding patterns in the image data to encode it more efficiently.

The newer WebP format combines both lossy and lossless compression techniques, often achieving 30% better compression than JPEG at similar quality levels. Our compressor tool intelligently applies the best compression techniques based on your chosen settings and image characteristics.

Best Practices for Optimal Results

  • Start with high-quality source images for the best compression results
  • For web use, aim for 70-85% quality for JPEGs
  • Use PNG for graphics with text, logos, or transparency needs
  • Consider converting older JPEGs to WebP for additional savings
  • Always test compressed images on different devices and screens
  • Keep original files before compression for future needs
