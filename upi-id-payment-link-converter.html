What is a UPI Payment Link?

A UPI payment link is a specially formatted URL (Uniform Resource Locator) that uses the upi://pay deep-link protocol. When a user clicks this link on a smartphone, their operating system automatically detects it and opens their preferred UPI application (such as Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, or BHIM). The app opens with your payment details—including the exact amount and payee name—already pre-filled. The user simply enters their UPI PIN to complete the transaction.

Understanding the Technical Architecture (The Deep Link)

For developers and tech enthusiasts, it is helpful to understand how the URL is actually constructed. The NPCI has standardized the UPI deep-linking specification. Here is a breakdown of the parameters our tool uses:

  • upi://pay - The protocol that tells the smartphone to look for a UPI-enabled app.
  • ?pa= - (Payee Address) Your actual UPI ID.
  • &pn= - (Payee Name) The name of the merchant or receiver.
  • &am= - (Amount) The transaction value in Indian Rupees (INR).
  • &tn= - (Transaction Note) The description or invoice reference.
  • &cu=INR - (Currency) Defaults to Indian Rupees.

Is it mandatory to enter an amount?

Why is my payment link not opening on my laptop?
UPI payment links (upi://pay) are designed specifically for mobile operating systems (Android and iOS) that have UPI apps installed. If you click the link on a desktop computer, it may show an error because desktop browsers do not have native UPI apps. On desktops, it is better to display the QR code so the user can scan it with their phone.
