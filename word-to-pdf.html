Why Converting Word to PDF is Usually So Hard

To understand why our tool is different, we must understand why most "free converters" fail.

1. The "Lost Logo" Problem

Microsoft Word uses a proprietary structure called XML. Headers and Footers (where you usually put School Logos or Letterheads) exist in a different "layer" of the file. Simple conversion scripts used by most free websites only read the "Body Text" layer. As a result, when you download your PDF, the body text is there, but your professional letterhead is gone.

2. The Regional Language Nightmare (Hindi/Marathi)

Scripts like Hindi, Marathi, Sanskrit, and Arabic rely on "Ligatures" (Matras and joint letters). For example, to write '‡§ï‡•ç‡§∑' (Ksha), the computer combines '‡§ï' + '‡§∑'. Standard PDF engines used by servers often lack the font libraries to do this math instantly. The result is "broken text" where letters are scattered or squares appear. Our tool uses the Google Chrome/Edge Font Rendering Engine, which is the gold standard for displaying world languages correctly.

3. The Layout Shift

Word documents flow text. PDFs have fixed text. If the conversion software estimates the page width wrong by even 10 pixels, a single line of text can spill over, creating an ugly 2-page document from a 1-page resume.

Troubleshooting: "My Logo isn't appearing!"

This is the most common query. If you use our tool and your school logo or background colors are still white, the setting lies in your Browser Print Dialog.

The Solution: When the "Save as PDF" window opens, look for "More Settings". You MUST check the box that says "Background Graphics". Browsers disable this by default to save printer ink, but for a PDF, you need it enabled!
