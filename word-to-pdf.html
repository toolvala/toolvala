Free Word to PDF Converter: A Secure, Client-Side Tool

Converting a Word document to PDF shouldn't mean uploading it to a stranger's server. This free Word to PDF converter runs entirely in your browser — no file leaves your device, there's no sign-up, and there's no daily limit. It also supports a text to PDF mode for whenever you just need to turn plain text into a clean, printable document.

Built for A4, A3 and US Letter output, with solid support for Hindi, Marathi and other non-Latin scripts, it's designed to handle the everyday paperwork of students, teachers and small offices without the usual friction of online converters.

Why a browser-based converter?

Most online converters work by uploading your file to a remote server, processing it there, and sending a result back. That's an extra hop your document doesn't need to take. This tool instead reads your file locally using JavaScript and builds the PDF on your own device.

Your files never leave your device

Because nothing is uploaded, there's no risk of a file sitting on a server you don't control. That matters most for contracts, mark sheets, ID documents, or anything you'd rather not send anywhere.

Works without an internet connection

Once the page has loaded, the converter keeps working even if you go offline — handy on a train, in an exam hall with patchy Wi-Fi, or anywhere bandwidth is tight.

No limits, no account

Convert one file or fifty — there's no quota, no watermark, and nothing to sign up for.

What this tool can do

  • Two input modes: upload a .docx file, or paste raw text directly.
  • Three page sizes: A4 (210×297mm), A3 (297×420mm) and US Letter (215.9×279.4mm).
  • Multi-script support: Hindi, Marathi, Arabic and other UTF-8 scripts render correctly — no "tofu" boxes.
  • Live preview: see exactly how the PDF will look before you download it.
  • Smart page breaks: the tool avoids slicing a paragraph, table row or list item across two pages wherever possible.

How to convert a Word file to PDF

  1. Upload: open the "Upload Word File" tab and drop your .docx into the box.
  2. Pick a size: choose A4, A3 or Letter from the dropdown.
  3. Convert: click "Convert & Preview" — the document is parsed and rendered on the page.
  4. Download: check the preview, then click "Download PDF".

Converting pasted text instead

Switch to the "Paste Text" tab, type or paste your content, and click convert. Paragraph breaks are preserved automatically, and long lines wrap cleanly within the page margins.

Fixing common formatting headaches

Why does text get cut off in other tools?

This usually happens when content is wider than the page. This converter enforces word wrapping inside a fixed-width page area, so long lines and long URLs break onto the next line instead of running off the edge.

Why do tables and paragraphs get sliced in half elsewhere?

Many converters split a page purely by height, ignoring what's actually on the page. This tool looks at where each paragraph, list item and table row sits before deciding where a page should end, and shifts the break to the nearest safe gap instead of cutting through a line of text.

Frequently asked questions

Is my document uploaded to a server?

No. The DOCX file is read directly in your browser using JavaScript, and the PDF is generated on your own device. Nothing is sent anywhere.

Which file formats can I convert?

You can upload .docx files, or paste plain text directly into the Paste Text tab. Older .doc files aren't supported — save them as .docx first.

Does it support Hindi and Marathi text?

Yes. The tool renders any UTF-8 text correctly, including Devanagari script used for Hindi and Marathi.

Can I choose A3 or US Letter instead of A4?

Yes, the page size dropdown lets you switch between A4, A3 and US Letter before converting.

Will my document's images and tables show up?

Yes. Embedded images and tables from your DOCX are carried over into the preview and the final PDF.

Is there a file size or page limit?

No artificial limit is enforced, though very large documents with many high-resolution images will naturally take longer to render.

Does this work on mobile?

Yes. On smaller screens, scroll sideways across the preview to see the full page width before downloading.

Do I need to install anything?

No installation is needed — it runs in any modern browser, and keeps working offline once the page has loaded.
