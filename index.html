Complete suite of tools for Teacher Eligibility Test preparation

What is TET?

  • Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) is a mandatory qualification
  • Required for teaching positions in schools
  • Conducted at both central (CTET) and state levels
  • Tests pedagogical knowledge and subject expertise

TET Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum 50% marks in graduation
  • B.Ed or equivalent teaching degree
  • Age limits vary by state
  • Reservation benefits as per government norms

Exam Pattern

  • Two Papers: I & II
  • Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • 150 marks each paper
  • No negative marking

TET Exam Scheme & Pattern

The Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) is conducted in two papers: Paper I for teachers of classes I-V and Paper II for teachers of classes VI-VIII. Both papers consist of 150 multiple-choice questions with no negative marking. The exam tests candidates on Child Development & Pedagogy, Language I & II, Mathematics, Environmental Studies (for Paper I), and Mathematics & Science or Social Studies (for Paper II).
