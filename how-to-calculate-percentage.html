1. What is a Percentage? The Basics

Before diving into the formulas, it helps to understand what a percentage actually is. The word "percent" is derived from the Latin phrase per centum, which literally translates to "by the hundred" or "for every hundred."

When you say "50 percent" (50%), you are essentially saying "50 out of 100."

  • 50% is equal to the fraction 50/100, which simplifies to 1/2.
  • In decimal form, 50% is written as 0.50.

Percentages are simply a standardized way to express a fraction of a whole, where the "whole" is always represented as 100.

2. The Master Formula: How to Calculate Percentage

The most basic and universally used formula to calculate a percentage when you know the "part" and the "whole" is:

Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100

Example: Calculating Event Attendance

You organized a webinar, and you sent out 250 invitations. On the day of the event, 180 people attended. What is the attendance percentage?

  1. Part: 180
  2. Whole: 250
  3. Step 1: Divide part by whole (180 ÷ 250 = 0.72)
  4. Step 2: Multiply by 100 (0.72 × 100 = 72)
  5. Answer: You had a 72% attendance rate.

4. How to Find "X Percent of Y" (Discounts and Taxes)

The second most common percentage problem is figuring out a specific percentage of a known number. You encounter this while shopping during a sale or calculating tax.

Formula

(Percentage ÷ 100) × Whole = Part

Shortcut: Just multiply the decimal form of the percentage by the whole number.

Example: Calculating a Store Discount

You are buying a jacket that originally costs ₹2,500, but there is a "30% Off" sale. How much is the discount, and what is the final price?

  1. Convert 30% to a decimal: 30 ÷ 100 = 0.30
  2. Multiply by the total price: 0.30 × 2500 = ₹750. (This is your discount)
  3. Subtract the discount from the original price: ₹2500 - ₹750 = ₹1,750.

5. Mental Math Tricks to Calculate Percentages Instantly

You won’t always have access to a calculator. Here are a few mental math shortcuts that will make you look like a mathematical genius:

Finding 10%

To find 10%, simply move the decimal point one place to the left.
Example: 10% of 450 = 45.0

Finding 5%

First, find 10% (by moving the decimal), and then cut that number in half.
Example: 10% of 600 is 60. Half is 30.

Finding 20%

Find 10% and double it.
Example: 10% of 800 is 80. Double it to get 160.

The Reversible Rule

x% of y is exactly the same as y% of x.
Example: 4% of 50 is hard. But 50% of 4 is easy (it's 2).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How do I calculate a percentage between two numbers?
To find out what percentage Number A is of Number B, divide A by B, and then multiply by 100. Formula: (A ÷ B) × 100. Example: What percentage of 80 is 20? (20 ÷ 80) = 0.25. Multiply by 100 = 25%.
How do you find the original price before a percentage discount?
To reverse a discount, you need to divide the final price by the remaining percentage. Example: A shirt costs ₹600 after a 20% discount. The remaining percentage you paid is 80% (or 0.80). To find the original price: 600 ÷ 0.80 = ₹750.
What does it mean if a percentage is over 100%?
A percentage over 100% simply means the amount has more than doubled or represents more than the original whole. For example, if a company’s profits were ₹10,000 last year and ₹25,000 this year, their profits are 250% of what they were last year.

How do I calculate profit margin percentage?
Profit margin calculates how much of your revenue is actual profit. Formula: ((Revenue - Cost) ÷ Revenue) × 100. Example: If you sell a product for ₹100 and it cost you ₹60 to make, your profit is ₹40. Margin = (40 ÷ 100) × 100 = 40% margin.
Is "x% of y" really the same as "y% of x"?
Yes, it is a proven mathematical property (the commutative property of multiplication). 15% of 50 (which is 7.5) yields the exact same result as 50% of 15 (which is 7.5). This trick is incredibly useful for mental math.
