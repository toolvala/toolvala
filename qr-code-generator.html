Deep Dive: How QR Codes Work

At its core, a QR code is a two-dimensional matrix barcode. It works by converting text or binary data into a grid of black and white squares (called modules). The Toolvala engine utilizes the ISO/IEC 18004 standard to ensure that every code you generate is readable by 100% of modern smartphone cameras.

Error Correction Levels (ECC)

Our tool implements high-level (Level H) error correction. This means that even if the printed QR code is 30% dirty, damaged, or obscured, the scanner can still reconstruct the data perfectly. This is vital for codes printed on outdoor signage, business cards, or packaging that might suffer from wear and tear.

Branding with Colors

Standard black and white codes are functional but boring. We provide full color customization. By matching the QR code to your brand's palette, you increase visual trust. High contrast is key—always ensure your foreground color is significantly darker than your background color to maintain scan reliability.

3. Why won't my QR code scan? ▼
The most common reason is low contrast. If your QR dots are light grey on a white background, cameras may struggle. Always use a high-contrast dark color for the dots.
4. How much data can a QR code hold? ▼
A standard QR code can hold up to 4,296 alphanumeric characters. However, for the best scanning speed, we recommend keeping your text or URL under 200 characters.
