What is the CTET Mock Test?

The CTET Mock Test is a simulated practice examination designed according to the latest CBSE guidelines for the Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET). It provides teaching aspirants with an authentic exam-like environment to test their knowledge, primarily focusing on Child Development and Pedagogy (CDP), which forms the core of both Paper 1 and Paper 2.

Why Practicing CTET Mock Tests is Crucial

  • Time Management: The actual exam gives you 150 minutes for 150 questions. Regular mock tests train your brain to read and solve questions within a 1-minute window.
  • Identifies Weak Areas: By reviewing the explanations for incorrect answers, you can pinpoint exactly which psychological theories or pedagogical concepts you need to revise.
  • Boosts Exam Confidence: Familiarity with the MCQ format and the trickiness of pedagogy questions eliminates exam anxiety.
  • Improves Accuracy: CTET questions often have options that look very similar. Practice helps you differentiate between the "good" option and the "most appropriate" option.

CTET Exam Pattern Overview (2026)

The CTET examination consists of two distinct papers:

  • Paper I: Mandatory for candidates intending to be teachers for Primary Classes (Class 1 to 5).
  • Paper II: Mandatory for candidates intending to be teachers for Upper Primary Classes (Class 6 to 8).

Both papers contain exactly 150 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) carrying 1 mark each. A major advantage of this exam is that there is no negative marking, meaning you should attempt every single question.

Tips to Score 110+ in CTET Exam

  • Master CDP: Child Development and Pedagogy is not just a 30-mark section. Its concepts apply to the pedagogical sections of Mathematics, Languages, and EVS/Science. Mastering CDP practically secures you 80-90 marks.
  • Read NCERT Textbooks: For Paper 1, read NCERT EVS and Math books from Class 3 to 5. For Paper 2, read your specific domain (Math/Science or SST) from Class 6 to 8.
  • Analyze Mistakes: Never just check your score and close the mock test. Read the explanations provided for the answers you got wrong.
