Why Convert Images to PDF?

Portable Document Format (PDF) is the global standard for document sharing. Here is why you should convert your JPGs and PNGs to PDF:

  • Universal Compatibility: PDFs look the same on every device, whether it's a smartphone, tablet, or desktop computer. No more "missing font" or layout issues.
  • Compact Sharing: Instead of attaching 10 separate image files to an email, you can send a single, organized PDF file.
  • Security: PDFs can be password-protected (using other tools) and are harder to edit than raw image files, ensuring your content remains intact.
  • Professionalism: Submitting a single PDF file for job applications, assignments, or invoices looks much more professional than a zip folder of images.

Supported Image Formats

Our tool supports the most common web image formats:

  • JPG/JPEG: The standard for photographs. Best for scanned documents and real-world photos.
  • PNG: Supports transparency and high quality. Ideal for screenshots, logos, and graphics.
  • WebP: The modern web format that offers superior compression and quality.

Tips for Best Results

  • Consistent Dimensions: For the best-looking PDF, try to use images with similar aspect ratios (e.g., all vertical or all horizontal).
  • Optimize Before Converting: If your images are huge (e.g., 10MB each), consider using our Image Compressor first to keep the final PDF size manageable.
  • Check Orientation: If you have a mix of portrait and landscape images, the "Fit to Image" page size option usually works best.
