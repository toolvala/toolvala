3. Why Age Calculation Matters for Exams (TET/CTET)

For aspiring teachers in India, age is a critical eligibility criterion. Most **TET (Teacher Eligibility Test)** notifications specify a "cutoff date"—meaning your age must be between 18 and 35 (or 40 for reserved categories) specifically as of that date, not just the day you fill out the form.

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How does the leap year affect my age? ▼
Leap years add a 29th day to February every 4 years. Our algorithm tracks these occurrences to ensure your total day count is precise and doesn't drift over long periods.
2. Is 18 years the universal minimum age for exams? ▼
In India, most state-level exams and TET/CTET require a minimum age of 18. Some specialized roles might require 21 years. Always check the specific official notification.
3. Can I use this tool for insurance policy applications? ▼
Yes. Insurance premiums are often calculated based on your 'nearest birthday' or 'exact age.' This tool provides the precise data required for insurance underwriting.
4. Why does the age sometimes differ on different websites? ▼
Differences occur when tools don't account for the current month's day count correctly. Toolvala uses the standard 'Borrowing' method from arithmetic to ensure 100% consistency.
5. Is my birth data saved on your server? ▼
No. Your privacy is paramount. All calculations happen locally on your phone or computer. We do not store or see your date of birth.
6. Can I calculate the age of a minor for school admission? ▼
Absolutely. Many schools require children to be at least 5 or 6 years old as of June 1st. You can set the 'As of Date' to June 1st to verify admission eligibility.
