What is Simple Interest?

Simple Interest is the easiest method of calculating interest on a loan or investment. It is calculated only on the original principal amount and does not include interest on interest.

Simple Interest Formula

SI = (P × R × T) / 100

Where P = Principal, R = Rate of Interest, T = Time (in years)

What is Simple Interest?

Simple Interest is the interest calculated on the principal amount for a specific period at a fixed rate. The interest remains the same every year because it is always calculated on the original principal.

Simple Interest Formula:

SI = (P × R × T) / 100

Where:

  • P = Principal amount
  • R = Rate of interest (per year)
  • T = Time period (in years)

Step-by-Step Example

Let us understand simple interest with a practical example:

  • Principal (P) = ₹10,000
  • Rate (R) = 5% per year
  • Time (T) = 2 years

Simple Interest = (10,000 × 5 × 2) / 100 = ₹1,000

Total Amount = Principal + Interest = ₹11,000

Simple Interest vs Compound Interest

Many people confuse simple interest with compound interest. The key difference is that compound interest is calculated on both the principal and the accumulated interest, while simple interest is calculated only on the principal.

For long-term investments, compound interest is more beneficial. However, for short-term borrowing and learning purposes, simple interest is preferred.
