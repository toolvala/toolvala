How to Create the Perfect TET Study Plan for 2026

Success in the Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) doesn't just come from hard work; it comes from smart planning. Whether you are preparing for CTET, UPTET, or REET, covering the vast syllabus of Child Development, Pedagogy, Languages, and Subject-specific topics requires a structured approach.

Pro Tip: The secret to cracking TET is scoring high in Child Development & Pedagogy (CDP). This subject accounts for 30 marks directly and influences pedagogy questions in all other sections.

Key Components of a TET Study Plan

  • CDP (30 Marks): Requires daily reading of theories (Piaget, Vygotsky, Kohlberg).
  • Language I & II (60 Marks): Focus on comprehension and grammar. Practice one unseen passage daily.
  • Main Subject (60 Marks): For Paper 1, this is Maths & EVS. For Paper 2, it is Maths/Science or Social Studies. This carries the highest weightage.

5-Step Strategy for TET 2026

  1. Analyze the Syllabus: Download the latest 2026 syllabus from the official website.
  2. Previous Year Papers: Solve at least one paper every weekend.
  3. Time Management: Use this calculator to split your day. Give more time to your weak subjects.
  4. Mock Tests: Attempt online mock tests to improve speed.
  5. Revision: Keep the last 15 days exclusively for revision. Do not start new topics then.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How many months are required to crack CTET?
An average student needs about 3 months of dedicated preparation (4 hours daily) to score 110+ in CTET.
Can I prepare for TET while doing a job?
Yes! Use the "2 Hours" option in the planner. Focus on weekends for mock tests and revision.
Which subject should I focus on first?
Start with Child Development & Pedagogy (CDP). It forms the base for understanding teaching methodologies in all other subjects.
