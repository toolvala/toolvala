Complete Guide to TET Marks Calculation & Qualifying Criteria (2026)

The Teacher Eligibility Test (TET) is the gateway to a teaching career in India. Whether you are appearing for the Central Teacher Eligibility Test (CTET) conducted by CBSE or various State TETs (like UPTET, REET, TNTET, MAHATET), understanding the marking scheme and qualifying criteria is essential. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about TET marks calculation, passing percentages, and validity rules for the year 2026.

Key Update 2026: The validity of the TET Qualifying Certificate remains Lifetime. Candidates do not need to re-appear for the exam every 7 years unless they wish to improve their score.

1. TET Exam Pattern & Marking Scheme

Almost all TET exams in India follow a standardized pattern set by the National Council for Teacher Education (NCTE). The exam is divided into two papers (Paper 1 for Primary and Paper 2 for Upper Primary), but the marking logic remains consistent.

  • Total Questions: 150 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).
  • Total Marks: 150 Marks.
  • Duration: 2.5 Hours (150 Minutes).
  • Marking Rule: +1 mark for every correct answer.
  • Negative Marking: Zero (0). There is no negative marking in CTET or most State TETs, which encourages candidates to attempt all questions.

2. Qualifying Marks for CTET & State TET 2026

To be declared "TET Qualified," a candidate must score a minimum percentage of marks. This threshold varies based on the candidate's category (General vs. Reserved).

Category Minimum Percentage Minimum Marks (Out of 150)
General (Unreserved) 60% 90 Marks
OBC (NCL) 55% 82 Marks
SC / ST 55% 82 Marks
PwD / Differently Abled 55% 82 Marks

Note on 82 Marks: Mathematically, 55% of 150 is 82.5. However, CBSE and state boards accept 82 marks as the passing score for reserved categories. There is no rounding off to 83 required.

3. How to Calculate TET Percentage?

Calculating your percentage is straightforward. You can use the tool above or the manual formula:

Percentage = (Marks Obtained / 150) × 100

Example: If you scored 105 marks:
(105 ÷ 150) × 100 = 70%.

4. CTET vs. State TET: Is the Criteria Different?

While the NCTE sets the baseline of 60% and 55%, some states have their own relaxation rules for domiciled candidates.

  • CTET: Strictly follows the 90 (Gen) and 82 (Reserved) rule.
  • UPTET: Follows the same 90/82 rule.
  • REET (Rajasthan): Often provides extra relaxation for widows, ex-servicemen, and Sahariya tribe candidates (sometimes lowering cutoff to 50% or even 36% for specific groups).
  • TSTET / APTET: Qualifying marks are often 60% (Gen), 50% (BC), and 40% (SC/ST). Check your specific state notification.

5. Importance of TET Score in Recruitment

Is TET just a qualifying exam, or does the score matter? This depends on the recruitment policy:

  • Qualifying Nature: For central government jobs (like KVS, NVS) and many states (like Delhi DSSSB), TET is just a qualifying exam. A candidate with 90 marks and a candidate with 140 marks are treated equally regarding eligibility.
  • Weightage Based: Some states (e.g., Uttar Pradesh for Super TET) add a percentage of your TET marks (e.g., 25% or 1/4th) to your final merit list. In such cases, scoring high is crucial, not just passing.

6. Validity & Re-attempts

Once you qualify, your certificate is valid for a lifetime. However, there is no limit on the number of attempts. If you have qualified with 95 marks but want to improve your score to 120+ for better recruitment prospects, you can re-appear in the next cycle.

7. How to Download TET Certificate?

For CTET, physical certificates are no longer sent. You must download your digitally signed certificate from DigiLocker using your Aadhar number. State TETs may still issue physical certificates or online provisional scorecards.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is the passing score for General Category in TET?
General category candidates need to score at least 90 marks out of 150, which equals 60%.
Is 82 marks pass or fail for OBC in CTET?
82 marks is a PASS. Although 55% of 150 is 82.5, the board considers 82 as the qualifying mark for OBC, SC, and ST candidates.
Can I apply for teaching jobs if I score 89 marks (General)?
No. For General category, strict 90 marks are required. 89 is considered "Not Qualified" unless you belong to a reserved category.
Is there negative marking in CTET 2026?
No, there is no negative marking. You should attempt all 150 questions.
Does the TET certificate expire?
No, the TET qualifying certificate now has Lifetime Validity across India.
