What Is a Rent Receipt?

A rent receipt is a written acknowledgment issued by a landlord to a tenant confirming that rent has been received for a specific month. It acts as legal proof of payment and is often required for income tax and accounting purposes.

A standard rent receipt in India includes tenant name, landlord name, property address, rent amount, rent period, date of issue, and landlord signature.

Why Rent Receipt Is Required for HRA Exemption

House Rent Allowance (HRA) is a salary component provided to salaried employees. To claim HRA exemption under Section 10(13A) of the Income Tax Act, employees must submit rent receipts to their employer.

  • Proof of actual rent paid
  • Mandatory for payroll HRA calculation
  • Required during income tax assessment
  • Helps avoid tax penalties

Is Rent Receipt Mandatory?

Yes. If you are claiming HRA exemption, submitting rent receipts is mandatory. If your annual rent exceeds ₹1,00,000, you must also provide your landlord’s PAN number as per income tax rules.

Valid Rent Receipt Format in India

A valid rent receipt format must contain:

  • Tenant full name
  • Landlord full name
  • Rental property address
  • Rent amount in INR
  • Month and year of rent
  • Date of issue
  • Landlord signature
