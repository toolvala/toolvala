3. Understanding CTC vs. In-Hand Salary

When you receive a hike letter, it usually mentions the increase in your Cost to Company (CTC). It is essential to realize that your "New Salary" as shown by our calculator is a gross figure. Your actual take-home amount will differ due to several factors common in Indian payroll systems:

  • Provident Fund (PF): Both employee and employer contributions increase as your basic salary goes up.
  • TDS (Income Tax): A higher salary might push you into a higher tax bracket. Check your liability with our Income Tax India Tool.
  • Professional Tax: A standard deduction based on your state's laws.
  • HRA Component: If your basic increases, your HRA eligibility also changes. Calculate your tax-free portion with our HRA Exemption Calculator.

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How is the increment percentage calculated? ▼
The formula is: [(New Salary - Current Salary) / Current Salary] * 100. Our tool automates this so you can see the results instantly without manual math.
2. Does the hike apply to the Basic salary or Gross? ▼
In most companies, the hike is applied to the total CTC. However, the distribution among Basic, HRA, and Special Allowance depends on your company's HR policy.
3. What is a "Comp-Ratio"? ▼
Comp-Ratio is your salary divided by the market midpoint for your role. If your ratio is low, you have a strong case for a higher hike than the standard company average.
4. Can I use this for hourly wages? ▼
Yes! Simply enter your hourly wage in the monthly box, and the results will show your new hourly rate. For dedicated wage conversion, use our Salary Converter.
5. Is my data secure on Toolvala.in? ▼
Absolutely. All calculations happen locally in your browser. We never store or see your salary details. Your financial privacy is 100% guaranteed.
6. What is a 'Retention Hike'? ▼
This is a salary increase offered when an employee has a competing job offer. It is often much higher than standard annual increments.
