Categorizing Your Expenses: Needs vs. Wants

When using our expense tracker, it is helpful to categorize your spending into two primary buckets:

1. Fixed Expenses (Needs)

These are the costs you must pay to survive and maintain your lifestyle. They include house rent, electricity, water, and insurance premiums. These are generally stable and harder to reduce in the short term.

2. Variable Expenses (Wants)

This category includes groceries, fuel, entertainment, and shopping. Because these fluctuate, they are the best place to start when you need to save money. Small changes in how you shop for groceries or how often you order food online can drastically improve your monthly surplus.

The "latte Factor": Small Leaks, Big Ships

Coined by financial author David Bach, the "Latte Factor" suggests that it's not the big purchases that break a budget, but the tiny, daily ones. A ₹200 daily coffee or a ₹300 evening snack adds up to nearly ₹9,000 a month. Our monthly spending tool calculates your "Daily Average" to help you visualize this impact.

Top 5 Strategies to Lower Monthly Expenses

  1. Automate Fixed Payments: Use UPI or bank mandates to ensure rent and utilities are paid on time, avoiding late fees.
  2. The 48-Hour Rule: Before any non-essential purchase, wait 48 hours. Most "wants" lose their appeal after two days.
  3. Audit Subscription Burn: Use our Subscription Budget Calculator to find hidden monthly charges you've forgotten about.
  4. Switch to Generic: Often, grocery store brands offer the same quality as name brands at a 30% lower price.
  5. Cook in Batches: Meal prepping reduces the urge to order expensive takeaway during busy workdays.
