2. How the Logic Works: The Greedy Algorithm

Our tool doesn't just guess which plates to use. It follows the **Standard Greedy Algorithm** used in professional weightlifting loaders:

  • Step 1: Subtract the Bar weight (45lb or 20kg) from your Target.
  • Step 2: Divide the remaining weight by 2 to isolate the load for one side.
  • Step 3: Identify the largest available plate (e.g., 45 lbs or 25 kg) and fit as many as possible into that weight.
  • Step 4: Move to the next smallest plate and repeat until the remainder is zero.

This ensures you always load the **minimum number of plates** possible, keeping the weight close to the bar's center of gravity (the collar), which reduces "bar whip" and increases stability during heavy lifts.

3. Lbs vs. Kg: Standardizing Your Lifts

Depending on your gym equipment, you may need to switch between units. Our calculator supports both:

The Pounds (Lbs) System

Standard in the USA and Canada, this system uses 45, 35, 25, 10, 5, and 2.5 lb plates. Note that 35lb plates are often considered "fake weight" in serious powerlifting gyms because they make the math harder and take up extra room on the tree. Our tool handles them seamlessly if you have them in your inventory.

The Kilograms (Kg) System

The global standard for Olympic Weightlifting and International Powerlifting (IPF). It uses a 20kg bar and plates ranging from 25kg down to 1.25kg. Our visualizer uses the official competition colors: Red (25), Blue (20), Yellow (15), and Green (10).

4. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Does the bar weight include the collars? ▼
Standard spring collars are negligible in weight. However, professional competition collars weigh exactly 2.5kg each. For high-precision lifting, you should add 5kg to your bar weight setting.
2. Why is it important to load heavy plates first? ▼
Loading heavy weight closest to the center of the bar reduces leverage against the shaft, which prevents unnecessary bending and keeps the center of mass stable.
3. What if I can't hit my exact target weight? ▼
If your target is 222 lbs but your smallest plate is 2.5 lbs, our tool will show a 'Remainder' warning. It suggests the closest possible weight you can achieve with standard equipment.
4. Can I use this for a 15kg Women's Bar? ▼
Yes! Simply switch the unit to KG and select the 15kg option from the Bar Weight dropdown menu.
5. Is the math different for a Smith Machine? ▼
Yes. Smith Machine bars are often counterbalanced and can weigh as little as 15 lbs. Always check the manufacturer's label on the machine before inputting the bar weight.
6. Is my lift data saved on Toolvala? ▼
No. Toolvala.in uses client-side processing. Your PRs and target weights are never sent to our servers. Your privacy is protected while you train.
