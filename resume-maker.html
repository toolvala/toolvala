Resume vs. CV: Whatâ€™s the Difference?

You might notice we have a CV Maker and a **Resume Maker**. While the terms are often used interchangeably, there are key differences:

A **Resume** (Curriculum Vitae in Latin means "course of life", but in the US "Resume" is used) is typically a one-to-two-page document that highlights your most relevant experience and skills for a specific job. It is concise and to the point.

A **CV** (Curriculum Vitae in Europe/Academic contexts) is a detailed document that lists every job, publication, award, and education history. It can be many pages long.

This tool is optimized for the **Resume format**: clean, concise, and impact-driven.

How to Write a Job-Winning Resume in 2025

1. The Professional Summary

Ditch the old "Objective" statement. In 2025, recruiters want a "Professional Summary". This is a 2-3 sentence elevator pitch at the top of your resume. Mention your current title, years of experience, and one major achievement. For example: "Detail-oriented Software Engineer with 5+ years of experience in full-stack development. Proven track record of reducing page load times by 40%."

2. Quantify Your Experience

When listing your work history, don't just list duties. List **achievements**. Use numbers to prove your value. Instead of saying "Managed a team," say "Managed a team of 10 developers and increased productivity by 20%."

3. Tailor Your Skills

Read the job description carefully. If they ask for "Python" and "Project Management," make sure those exact keywords appear in your Skills section. Our tool allows you to easily add comma-separated tags to highlight these keywords.

4. Keep it Visual but Professional

While design matters, clarity is king. Avoid clutter. Use bullet points. Ensure there is enough white space. Our template uses a "Classic Modern" design with a subtle blue accent color, which research shows conveys trust and professionalism.
