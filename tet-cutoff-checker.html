What is TET Cutoff?

The TET cutoff is the minimum qualifying mark required to pass the Teacher Eligibility Test (TET). Candidates scoring equal to or above the cutoff are considered TET qualified.

The cutoff is decided by the exam authority and is based on NCTE guidelines. Cutoff marks may vary slightly for different state TET exams.

CTET Cutoff Marks 2026

For CTET, the qualifying marks are generally:

  • General Category: 60% (90 out of 150)
  • OBC / SC / ST: 55% (82 out of 150)

These cutoff marks apply to both Paper I (Class 1–5) and Paper II (Class 6–8).

State TET Cutoff Marks

Most state TET exams follow CTET-like cutoff rules. However, some states may provide additional relaxation to reserved categories.

Examples of State TET exams:

  • MAHA TET
  • UP TET
  • REET
  • TSTET
  • AP TET
