Merge Photo and Signature Online — Complete Guide

Applying for government jobs and entrance exams in India has moved entirely online. While convenient, it brings a common technical challenge: many portals require a single image containing both your passport photo and signature. Exam boards like GATE, AIIMS, and several State PSCs use this "joined" format to streamline admit card generation.

Toolvala.in's Photo & Signature Merger solves this in seconds — no Photoshop, no MS Paint, no confusion. Just upload, adjust, and download.

🔒 Privacy First: All processing happens locally in your browser. Your images are never uploaded to any server. Zero data retention.

Why Do Exams Require a Merged Photo-Signature Image?

  • Database Efficiency: One file per candidate reduces server storage compared to two separate files.
  • Admit Card Automation: Software places one image block (photo + sign) instead of calculating alignment for two separate elements.
  • Identity Verification: A combined image links your face to your signature in a single verifiable document.

How to Use This Tool — Step by Step

Step 1: Upload Both Images

Click the Upload Photo box to select your passport-size photo. Then click Upload Signature to select your scanned or photographed signature. Both boxes accept JPG, PNG, and WEBP files.

Step 2: Live Preview Appears Instantly

As soon as both images are selected, the tool renders a live merged preview on the right. No button click needed — it updates in real time as you adjust any setting.

Step 3: Adjust Layout Settings

Use Vertical orientation to stack photo on top with signature below (most common). Use Horizontal for side-by-side layout. Drag the Signature Scale slider to resize the signature relative to the photo, and the Gap slider to control spacing.

Step 4: Set Output Width and Quality

Select output width (350px, 500px, or 800px) based on the portal's requirement. Adjust the quality slider to control file size — the live KB badge turns red if the file exceeds 50KB.

Step 5: Download

Click Download Merged Image. The file saves as a JPG to your device, ready for upload to any exam portal.

Vertical vs. Horizontal — Which to Use?

Vertical (Stacked): Photo on top, signature below. Used in 95% of cases — SSC, IBPS, Railway RRB, University admissions.
Horizontal (Side-by-Side): Photo left, signature right. Used for wide-format forms, bank ledgers, or attendance sheets.

When in doubt, check the official notification or use vertical — it is the standard format.

Tips for Best Results

  • Photo: Use a white or light blue background, even lighting, and a clear front-facing shot.
  • Signature: Sign with a blue or black pen on a clean white sheet. Avoid ruled paper.
  • Crop tightly: Crop your signature image close — large white borders make the signature appear small after merging.
  • Scan at 200 DPI: If you have a scanner, 200 DPI gives a good balance of quality and file size for signatures.
⚠️ Common Mistake: If your signature image has excessive white space around it, it will look tiny in the merged output. Crop it tightly before uploading.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this Photo & Signature Merger tool free? ▼

Yes, 100% free with no registration, no subscription, and absolutely no watermarks on your downloaded image. Use it unlimited times.

Are my photos and signature safe? ▼

Completely safe. All image processing runs inside your browser using HTML5 Canvas. No image is ever sent to Toolvala.in's servers or any third-party service. Your personal documents remain private.

The merged image looks blurry. How do I fix it? ▼

Blurriness usually means the original images are low resolution. Upload higher-resolution source images and set the Quality slider to 0.9 or higher. Choosing a larger output width (500px or 800px) also helps.

Does it work on mobile phones? ▼

Yes. The tool is fully responsive. You can take a photo of your signature using your phone camera, pick a passport photo from your gallery, merge them, and download — all without installing any app.

The file size is still above the limit after compression. What can I do? ▼

Try reducing the Output Width to 350px and sliding the Quality to 0.4–0.6. You can also use Toolvala.in's Compress by KB tool to hit an exact target file size.

What image formats are supported? ▼

You can upload JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP, and GIF images. The download is always in JPG format, which is the format accepted by most government and exam portals.
