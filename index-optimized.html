What is a Photo and Signature Merge Tool?

This merged image is commonly required when filling out government applications, examination registration forms, university admissions, bank account opening forms, and many other official documents that ask you to submit a combined photo-signature image rather than uploading them separately.

Why Do Official Forms Require Photo and Signature on One Image?

Many government portals, banking institutions, and universities in India require applicants to upload a single image that contains both the photo and signature. This is done for identity verification purposes — it ensures that the person applying is the same person whose signature is present. Forms like the SSC CGL registration, Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) applications, and bank job portals commonly specify a combined photo-signature image requirement with strict file size and dimension limits.

Creating such an image manually — using tools like Photoshop or Paint — can be confusing and time-consuming, especially for those not familiar with image editing software. That's exactly why a free, easy-to-use tool like Toolvala.in's Photo Signature Merger is so valuable.

Tips for Getting the Best Merged Image

To produce a clean, professional-looking merged image that meets official form requirements, follow these best practices:

  • Use a photo with a plain white or light-colored background for a professional look
  • Ensure your signature is on a white background — scan it on plain white paper for best results
  • Use the "Side by Side" layout for most government and bank form requirements
  • Adjust the canvas width and photo height to match the exact pixel dimensions specified in the form guidelines
  • Download as JPG for most official form submissions, as it results in smaller file sizes
  • Download as PNG if the form requires a transparent background or high-quality image
  • Check the file size after download — most portals accept files under 50KB to 200KB

Optimizing Signature Background

If your scanned signature has a yellowish or grey background due to scanning, use the PNG format with a transparent background option. Alternatively, you can pre-process your signature image using a free background remover before uploading it to this tool. This ensures the signature blends seamlessly into the merged image.

Photo & Signature Specifications for Common Exams

Different competitive exams and official portals have different specifications for merged photo-signature images. Here are some common requirements:

  • SSC CGL / CHSL: Photo (3.5cm × 4.5cm), Signature (3.5cm × 1.5cm), combined image typically under 100KB JPG
  • IBPS Bank Exams: Photo (200×230 pixels), Signature (140×60 pixels) uploaded separately or combined
  • UPSC Civil Services: Photo JPG under 300KB, high-quality white background with formal dress
  • State PSC Exams: Varies by state — typically 200×230px photo and 140×60px signature
  • Railway RRB: Photo with clear white background, signature on white paper, both under 50KB

Always refer to the official notification for exact specifications. Toolvala.in's tool gives you complete control over dimensions, layout, and quality to match any requirement.
