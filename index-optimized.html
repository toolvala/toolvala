Introduction

In the digital age, PDF (Portable Document Format) is the gold standard for sharing official records, such as **Police Recruitment Merit Lists**, SSC/UPSC results, and bank statements. However, these documents often come with a high page count and large file sizes (often 5MB to 20MB). This poses a problem when you need to upload them to government portals (like MahaIT or SSC) that strictly enforce a 200kb or 500kb file size limit.

The **Toolvala PDF Compressor** is a high-end, browser-side utility designed specifically to solve this issue. By using advanced rasterization and DPI downsampling, our tool shrinks your files while ensuring the names and marks on recruitment lists remain sharp and legible. Best of all, because the tool works entirely in your browser, your sensitive data—like candidate names and application numbers—is never uploaded to any remote server.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. Will the compressed PDF be searchable? ▼
To achieve maximum compression for strict portal limits, this tool uses a 'Raster-to-PDF' method. This means the final PDF is an image-based layer. It is highly readable but may not allow text selection. For searchable PDFs, keep the quality slider at maximum.
2. How do I reach exactly 200kb? ▼
Start at the 'Medium' setting. If the result is over 200kb, re-process with the slider moved further to the left. Since processing is local and free, you can try different settings until you reach the target.
3. Can I compress multiple recruitment lists at once? ▼
We currently process one file at a time to ensure your browser does not crash. For multiple files, we recommend using our PDF Merger tool first.
4. Is there a watermark on my documents? ▼
No. Toolvala.in is a community resource. We never add watermarks or branding to your official documents.
5. My PDF is password protected. Can I compress it? ▼
For security, you must remove the password from your PDF using a PDF editor before uploading it to our browser-side compressor.
6. Does compression affect my mobile data? ▼
Minimal data is used to load the tool itself. Since the actual compression happens on your phone/computer, no data is consumed for "uploading" or "downloading" the file contents.
