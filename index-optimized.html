2. To Photo or Not to Photo?

Adding a **profile photo to your resume** is a decision that depends heavily on your geographic location and industry. Here is the breakdown of modern standards in 2026:

  • Europe & Asia: In many European countries (Germany, France) and across Asia (India, Singapore), a professional photo is often expected and helps build personal rapport.
  • USA & UK: Traditionally, photos are discouraged in these regions to prevent unconscious bias. However, for creative, sales, or public-facing roles, a visual presence is becoming more accepted.

Our tool gives you the flexibility. If you upload a photo, the layout adjusts beautifully. If you choose not to, the text expands to fill the space perfectly.

3. Mastering the ATS (Applicant Tracking System)

Over 75% of resumes are rejected by an automated bot before a human eye ever touches them. To win in 2026, your document must be **ATS-Friendly**. Toolvala's Resume Maker adheres to these critical rules:

  • Standard Headings: We use universal terms like "Work Experience" rather than "My Journey" to ensure the parser correctly categorizes your data.
  • Zero-Graph Philosophy: We avoid complex charts or progress bars which often confuse older ATS systems.
  • Standard Fonts: We use web-safe, professional fonts (Inter & Merriweather) that are easily readable by OCR technology.

4. Writing Impact-Driven Content (The STAR Method)

The biggest mistake job seekers make is listing "Tasks" instead of "Results." Recruiters don't want to know what you did; they want to know what you achieved. Use the **STAR Method** for your experience bullet points:

  1. Situation: The context of your task.
  2. Task: What was the goal?
  3. Action: What *specific* steps did you take?
  4. Result: What was the outcome? (Always use numbers!)

Example: "Increased regional sales by 25% within 6 months by implementing a new CRM strategy."
