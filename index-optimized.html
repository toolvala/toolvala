2. Resume vs. CV: Which Do You Need?

One of the most common questions job seekers ask is the difference between a Resume and a CV. While often used interchangeably, they serve different purposes depending on the region and industry.

  • The Resume: A concise document (usually 1-2 pages) used primarily in the US and India for corporate roles. It focuses on relevant skills and recent achievements tailored to a specific job description.
  • The CV (Curriculum Vitae): A detailed, multi-page record of your entire career, including academic research, publications, and awards. It is the standard for medical, academic, and scientific roles.

4. Section-by-Section Strategy

The Header & Professional Title

Your name should be the largest text on the page. Beneath it, your professional title should act as a "Headline." Don't just put your current job; put the job you are applying for. For example, if you are a "Project Coordinator" applying for a "Project Manager" role, use "Project Management Professional" to immediately signal your eligibility.

The Professional Summary

Ditch the "Objective" statement. Objectives are about what *you* want. Summaries are about what *you offer*. In 2-3 sentences, highlight your total years of experience, your core domain, and your single most impressive achievement (e.g., "Increased sales by 40% in Q3").

Quantifying Your Experience

Recruiters love numbers. Instead of saying "Responsible for managing a team," say "Managed a team of 15 engineers across 3 time zones to deliver a $2M project 2 weeks ahead of schedule." Numbers provide scale and credibility to your claims. Use the STAR Method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to write these bullet points.
