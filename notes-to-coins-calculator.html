Denomination Strategy: Maximizing Efficiency

A professional cashier doesn't just count; they optimize. Here is the standard priority list for a healthy cash drawer:

  • The Core: Keep a high volume of ₹500 and ₹200 notes. These are the most commonly accepted high-value denominations for large transactions.
  • The Change: Ensure a 1:10 ratio for smaller notes. For every one ₹500 note, you should ideally have ten ₹50 notes to facilitate easy change-giving.
  • Coin Management: Coins for ₹1, ₹2, and ₹5 are the final step in precision. Our calculator includes decimal/paise support to ensure you never lose a single cent in your accounting.

Top 5 Tips for Cashiers & Business Owners

  1. Face the Notes: Always keep notes facing the same way. It makes counting faster and reveals counterfeit or damaged notes more easily.
  2. Batch Counting: Group notes into bundles of 10 or 20 and secure them with a rubber band. Enter these "batches" into our calculator for rapid tallying.
  3. The Remainder Check: When using the "Auto Breakdown" for a figure like ₹1,34,553, always start by fulfilling the smallest units (coins/paise) first to ensure you have the exact change.
  4. Security First: Never tally large sums of cash in a public-facing area. Use the Toolvala.in mobile-responsive interface to tally cash in a secure back office.
  5. Digital Archives: Instead of keeping piles of paper slips, take a screenshot of your Toolvala result and save it in a "Daily Tally" folder on your computer.
