Understanding the Greedy Algorithm

The math behind our calculator follows the "Greedy Strategy." This means the tool always tries to use the largest possible denomination first. For example, if the amount is ₹650:

  • It first checks for ₹2000 (0 needed).
  • It checks for ₹500 (1 needed, remainder ₹150).
  • It checks for ₹200 (0 needed).
  • It checks for ₹100 (1 needed, remainder ₹50).
  • It checks for ₹50 (1 needed, remainder ₹0).
This ensures you always get the result using the minimum number of items, which is the gold standard in banking and retail.

Top 5 Cash Handling Pro-Tips

  1. The Face-Up Rule: Always keep your banknotes facing the same direction and in ascending order. It prevents counting errors and looks professional.
  2. Audit Regularly: Use our calculator to perform mid-day audits of your cash drawer to ensure no mistakes have been made during busy periods.
  3. Separate Large Bills: Keep high-value notes (like ₹500 or $100) separate from small change to avoid accidentally giving them back as change.
  4. Digital Tally: Keep a digital log of your breakdowns. Our tool saves your last entry locally, so you can come back and check it later.
  5. Sanitize: Physical currency changes hands thousands of times. Always use hand sanitizer after performing a manual cash breakdown.
